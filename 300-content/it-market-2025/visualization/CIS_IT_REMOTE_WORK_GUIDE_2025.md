## EXECUTIVE SUMMARY

The CIS IT market in 2025 faces significant challenges from international sanctions against Russia and Belarus, creating a bifurcated job search strategy:

1. **Restricted Countries (Russia/Belarus)**: Must use VPN/relocation strategies or crypto payments
2. **Open Markets (Kazakhstan, Kyrgyzstan, Uzbekistan, Armenia, Georgia)**: Direct access to international platforms
3. **Success Rate**: CIS developers can access remote work through outsourcing companies (40-60% hiring), niche platforms, and relocation strategies
4. **Payment Reality**: Crypto adoption has become standard; Wise/Payoneer blocked; traditional bank transfers difficult

### 1.1 For Russia/Belarus Residents (Requires Workarounds)

#### **Fiverr** (Available with Technical Solutions)
- **Reality**: Residents can access via VPN, but payment withdrawal is severely limited
- **Requirements**: Foreign IP address for registration, virtual phone number (services like Twillio), Payoneer or crypto wallet
- **Workaround Success Rate**: 40-50% successfully set up (high barrier to entry)
- **Commission**: 20% standard, 10% for top sellers
- **Action Items**:
  - Use legitimate VPN to residential IP in EU/US
  - Create account with virtual phone number
  - Use crypto wallet or Payoneer (with pre-existing account) for withdrawals
  - Keep account low-profile (no mentions of Russia/Belarus)

#### **Local Russian Platforms** (Safest Option)
- **Habr Career**: Russian-language platform, widely used by Russian developers
- **Freelance.ru**: Ruble-based payments, Russian clients

### 3.2 Actual Solutions Used by CIS Developers (2025)

#### **Option 1: Relocation (Kazakhstan/Kyrgyzstan/Uzbekistan) - 40% of CIS Developers**
- **Cost**: $200-500/month additional living cost
- **Timeline**: 2-4 weeks visa processing
- **Unlock**: Full access to Wise, direct bank transfers, no VPN needed
- **Action**:
  1. Apply for digital nomad visa (Armenia: $240 first year; Georgia: $200; Kazakhstan: varies)
  2. Move to Almaty, Bishkek, or Tashkent
  3. Open local bank account (3-5 days)
  4. Register with job platforms from new location
  5. Receive payments to local account
  6. Convert to crypto or use local forex services

#### **Option 2: Cryptocurrency (45-55% of CIS Developers Active)**
- **Primary Crypto Currencies**: Bitcoin, Ethereum, USDC, Ripple (XRP), Monero (privacy)
- **No-KYC Exchanges**: Over 100 operating in CIS in 2025
- **Exchange Rate**: 0.5-2% markup vs. official rate
- **Withdrawal Time**: 24-48 hours to fiat
- **Action**:
  1. Create Kraken account (allows Russia/Belarus signup)
  2. Receive payment in USDC (preferred: low volatility)
  3. Withdraw to private wallet
  4. Use P2P exchange or ATM for cash
  5. Convert in local bank if possible, or use crypto debit card

**Top Crypto Platforms Accepting CIS (2025)**:
- Kraken (still accepting Russian accounts)
- Bybit (supporting CIS users)
- OKX (operating in CIS despite pressure)
- Binance (partial access; requires workarounds)
- LocalBitcoins (peer-to-peer, no verification)
- Monero (privacy coin, harder to track)

#### **Option 3: Payoneer (Pre-2024 Account Only)**
- **Reality**: No new accounts for Russia since March 2024
- **For Existing Accounts**: 40% still functioning
- **Withdrawal Method**: Only to local bank accounts (cannot withdraw to Mastercard outside Russia)
- **Commission**: 1.95% + flat fee per withdrawal
- **Action** (if existing account):
  1. Keep account low-profile
  2. Receive payments from freelance platforms
  3. Withdraw exclusively to Russian bank account
  4. Convert to crypto or cash withdrawal at bank

#### **Option 4: Intermediary Services (Armenia/Georgia)**
- **Cost**: 5-15% commission
- **Timeline**: 3-7 days
- **How It Works**:
  1. Freelancer in Armenia/Georgia receives payment to their account
  2. They send wire transfer to your account in Russia/Belarus
  3. Small commission charged (5-15%)
- **Services**: EasierTransfer, SendFriend, or personal networks
- **Action**: Search "Kazakhstan bank transfer to Russia" or "Georgia freelance intermediary"

### 7.3 CIS Developer Interview Strategy

**Pre-Interview (1 week before)**:
1. Research company tech stack (check their GitHub, tech blog)
2. Study their product deeply (use as customer)
3. Prepare 3-4 questions about their engineering challenges
4. Record yourself explaining your portfolio (check pronunciation, flow)

**During Interview**:
1. **Technical Screen** (45 min, 1-2 problems):
   - Slow down; think out loud
   - Write pseudocode first
   - Explain approach before coding
   - Test your code (catches 30% of candidates)

2. **System Design** (45-60 min, 1 problem):
   - Ask clarifying questions first (10 min)
   - Draw architecture (whiteboard or excalidraw)
   - Discuss tradeoffs (don't just pick one solution)
   - Be ready to defend your choices

3. **Behavioral Interview**:
   - Tell stories, not features
   - Prepare 3-4 STAR format stories:
     * Difficult bug you fixed
     * Time you worked with non-technical person
     * Time you failed and learned
     * Time you optimized performance

4. **Final Questions**:
   - Ask about their team's biggest technical challenge
   - Ask about career growth (shows interest)
   - Ask about team composition (gauges health)

**Post-Interview**:
- Send thank you email within 2 hours
- Mention specific tech challenge you discussed
- Reiterate interest and availability
