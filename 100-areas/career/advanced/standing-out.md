# Standing Out: как стать заметным в море кандидатов

250+ заявок на позицию. ATS отсеивает 75%. Recruiter тратит 7 секунд на резюме. Hiring manager видит 5-10 кандидатов. Как оказаться среди них? Не быть одним из 250 — быть единственным, кого помнят.

---

## Проблема: все выглядят одинаково

```
ТИПИЧНЫЙ КАНДИДАТ:
• "Senior Android Developer with 7 years experience"
• "Proficient in Kotlin, Java, MVVM"
• "Team player, problem solver, fast learner"
• Resume из шаблона

ПОЧЕМУ НЕ РАБОТАЕТ:
• 50 других написали то же самое
• Ничего memorable
• Generic skills everyone claims
• No proof, only claims
```

---

## 5 способов выделиться

### 1. Solve Their Specific Problem

```
ВМЕСТО:
"I'm a Senior Android Developer looking for new opportunities"

СДЕЛАЙ:
1. Исследуй конкретную проблему компании
   • Их блоги, подкасты, конференции
   • Glassdoor reviews (pain points)
   • GitHub issues в их проектах

2. Предложи решение
   "I noticed [Company] is migrating to Compose based on
   your recent blog post. I led a similar migration at [X]
   that reduced UI code by 35%. Would love to share our
   approach and learn about your journey."

3. Покажи, не рассказывай
   • Link to your migration guide
   • Demo of similar work
   • Specific metrics you achieved

РЕЗУЛЬТАТ: Ты решаешь их проблему, не продаёшь себя.
```

### 2. Build Visible Expertise

```
ДО ПОДАЧИ ЗАЯВКИ:

Content:
• Blog posts о твоих технических решениях
• Open source contributions
• Conference talks (даже local meetups)
• StackOverflow ответы

Visibility:
• LinkedIn posts с insights
• Twitter/X tech threads
• YouTube tutorials

Proof:
• GitHub repos с звёздами
• Apps в Play Store
• Testimonials от коллег

ЭФФЕКТ:
Hiring manager гуглит тебя → находит твой контент
→ уже знает о твоей экспертизе → interview проще

"I know your work" >> "Tell me about yourself"
```

### 3. Personalize Aggressively

```
GENERIC:
"Dear Hiring Manager,
I am excited to apply for the Android Developer position..."
(автоматически в отказ)

PERSONALIZED:
"Hi Sarah,

I saw your talk at Droidcon about migrating from RxJava to Coroutines.
Your point about gradual migration really resonated —
we did something similar at [Company] using a hybrid approach.

I noticed [Company] is hiring for Android. Given my experience
with [specific thing matching their needs], I think I could
contribute to [specific team/project they mentioned publicly].

Would love to chat about how I might help.

Best,
[Name]"

ЧТО ПЕРСОНАЛИЗИРОВАТЬ:
• Имя hiring manager / team lead
• Конкретный проект или challenge компании
• Недавние публичные события (funding, launch)
• Referral если есть

RESEARCH TIME: 30 мин на одну заявку >> 30 generic заявок
```

### 4. Create a "Contribution Portfolio"

```
НЕ ПРОСТО РЕЗЮМЕ, А ПОРТФОЛИО ЦЕННОСТИ:

1. Tailored one-pager:
   • Specific problem they have
   • How you've solved similar problems
   • 3 concrete examples with metrics

2. Technical artifact:
   • Blog post addressing их challenge
   • Prototype/demo related to их domain
   • Architecture diagram solving их problem

3. Video intro:
   • 2-3 minute personal pitch
   • Shows communication skills
   • Memorable (few do this)

ПРИМЕР:
Вместо резюме отправь:
"Here's a technical brief on how I'd approach your Compose migration,
based on my experience leading [similar project]. Happy to discuss."

+ Link to 2-page document
+ Link to video walkthrough
```

### 5. Leverage Warm Introductions

```
COLD APPLY:
Resume → ATS → Maybe recruiter → Probably lost

WARM INTRO:
Referral → Hiring manager directly → Interview fast-tracked

КАК ПОЛУЧИТЬ WARM INTRO:

1. First-degree connections:
   "Hey [Name], I saw [Company] is hiring Android devs.
   Would you feel comfortable referring me?"

2. Second-degree connections:
   "Hi [Name], I see we're both connected to [Mutual].
   Would love to learn about your work at [Company]."

3. Alumni networks:
   Same university, bootcamp, previous company

4. Community connections:
   Met at conference, Slack community, open source

СТАТИСТИКА:
• Referred candidates: 1 in 16 get hired
• Cold applicants: 1 in 152 get hired
• 10x higher success rate with referral
```

## Messaging That Works

### LinkedIn Outreach

```
ПЛОХО:
"Hi, I'm interested in opportunities at [Company].
Do you have any openings?"

ХОРОШО:
"Hi [Name], I've been following [Company]'s work on [specific project].
Your approach to [specific thing] is impressive.

I'm currently exploring senior roles where I can contribute to
[specific challenge they have]. Based on your experience,
would you say [Company] values [specific trait you have]?

Happy to grab virtual coffee if you have 15 min."

КЛЮЧЕВЫЕ ЭЛЕМЕНТЫ:
• Specific observation (shows research)
• Low-commitment ask (question, not job request)
• Value-forward (interested in them, not just job)
```

### Cover Letter (если требуется)

```
СТРУКТУРА (3 paragraphs):

HOOK:
"[Company]'s mission to [X] resonates with me because [personal connection].
When I saw the [Role] opening, I knew my experience with [specific skill]
could contribute to [specific team goal]."

PROOF:
"At [Previous Company], I faced a similar challenge: [their problem].
I [specific action] which resulted in [metric]. I believe this
experience directly applies to [their context]."

CLOSE:
"I'd love to discuss how my background in [X] could help [Company]
achieve [their goal]. Available for a call this week."

---

ДЛИНА: 150-200 слов max
ТОНАЛЬНОСТЬ: Confident, not desperate
ФОКУС: Them, not you
```

## Pre-Application Checklist

```
ПЕРЕД КАЖДОЙ ЗАЯВКОЙ:

□ Research (30 min minimum):
  • Company blog, recent news
  • Team members на LinkedIn
  • Glassdoor reviews
  • GitHub/tech blog

□ Find connection:
  • Any 1st/2nd degree contacts?
  • Alumni from same school/company?
  • Community overlap?

□ Personalize resume:
  • Keywords from JD
  • Relevant experience highlighted
  • Metrics that matter to them

□ Personalize outreach:
  • Specific person to contact
  • Tailored message
  • Value proposition clear

□ Contribution artifact:
  • Something beyond resume
  • Shows you understand their problem
  • Demonstrates your approach
```

## Common Mistakes

```
❌ "I'm passionate about coding"
   → Everyone says this. Show, don't tell.

❌ "I'm a quick learner"
   → Proof: "Learned [X] in 2 weeks, shipped [Y]"

❌ "I have experience with [long list]"
   → Focus: 3-4 things most relevant to THIS role

❌ "I want to work at [Company] because it's a great company"
   → Specific: "[Company]'s approach to [X] aligns with how I work"

❌ Generic LinkedIn connect without message
   → Always add personalized note

❌ Applying to every opening at same company
   → Choose one, be specific
```

## The "Triple Threat" Skill Set

```
Что отличает top candidates в 2025:

1. TECHNICAL EXPERTISE:
   • Deep, not just wide
   • Proven with examples
   • Current (Compose, KMP, not just XML)

2. DOMAIN KNOWLEDGE:
   • Understand the business, not just code
   • Industry-specific experience
   • Product thinking

3. COMMUNICATION:
   • Can explain complex things simply
   • Written + verbal
   • Async communication (remote-essential)

SELF-CHECK:
Можешь ли ты объяснить:
• Technical decision hiring manager поймёт?
• Why your solution matters for business?
• How you'd approach their specific problem?

Если да на все три → ты выделяешься.
```
